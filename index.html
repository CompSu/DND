What is Dungeons & Dragons?

Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) is a fantasy tabletop role-playing game (RPG) originally designed by Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson. It was first published in 1974 and has since become the most popular and well-known RPG. Players assume the roles of characters in a fantasy world, solving quests, fighting monsters, and exploring vast lands, all under the guidance of a Dungeon Master (DM). In Dungeons & Dragons, the players form an adventuring party who explore fantasy worlds together, embark on epic quests, and level up. The Dungeon Master (also known as the DM) is the game's referee and lead storyteller. The collective creativity in your D&D game builds stories that you’ll tell again and again—ranging from the stuff of legend to absurd incidents that’ll make you laugh.

History of D&D

Dungeons & Dragons is a tabletop role-playing game that appeared in 1974. Its creators are Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson. At first, the game was a large-scale modification of the wargame Chainmail. But later it became an independent game due to a huge number of innovations and a shift in emphasis from strategic battles to role-playing. Dungeons & Dragons had a huge influence on the development of the fantasy genre. It gave us iconic monsters, recognizable spells, and the visual language of fantasy itself.
